In the beginning, the world was created by Aethel, the Creator. He made unto himself seven servants and companions, that he might rest from his labors; these are often called gods, to distinguish them from the God Aethel. There was Thera, who loved living things and the earth; Sylas, wild, free, and joyful; Valerius, the divine Warrior, protector of the innocent and judge of the wicked; and there were four more, whose names are now unknown; for so long ago did they forsake the path of righteousness, turning to their own corrupt ends. Chief of them in evil is Malakor, the Shadow, who seeks to dominate above all. Beyond this there is Vexa, the Whisperer, who deceives; Gorruk, the Beast, who seeks to savagely devour, and Nyxara, the Void, who seeks to merely cause despair.


The Age of Dawn was broken by the rebellion of the Dark Chorus, as some call the four lieutenants who rebelled. Thus, for millennia, four gods of darkness have struggled against three of light. While none of them act by the direct use of their own power, they each have many servants through whom they touch the world.
